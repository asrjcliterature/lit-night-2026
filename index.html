Lit Night

Anderson Serangoon Junior College

From the Cast & Crew

Behind the Curtain

"Lit Night is my first acting experience. I've been learning how to work the stage, how to improvise and adapt on the spot, and even how to stall — it's a skill! Most significantly, this has been a bonding experience with everyone, and being given the creative freedom to write my own script felt like something I'll look back on in ten years. My message to the audience: give everything a shot, because most of us performing are first-time actors."
Bhargavi
Emcee (Aphrodite) & Performer
"Lit Night is one of the most meaningful events in the Literature programme because it allows students to experience Literature as a living, collaborative art form rather than simply something studied on a page. Most of all, Lit Night is about legacy and tradition — sharing and passing down the love for Literature to each cohort of students. I hope the audience leaves more curious about Literature and sees its value in our society today."
Ms Chew
Teacher-in-Charge
"Literature is quite fun — there are a lot of musicals, funny acts, and romantic pieces. Lit Night is very enjoyable. I've been learning how to project myself and be more confident. At its heart, it's an appreciation for literature and the humanities."
Amir
Performer
"Lit Night is a fantastic opportunity for students to showcase their ability and develop their creative and leadership skills. Before this, I wasn't particularly curious about theatre production, but taking that first step taught me so much. This year, Lit Night explores themes we don't often talk about in real life: relationships, conversations, daily experiences. Watching it lets you analyse them from an outsider's perspective."
Sona
Student Director
"This was my first time properly experiencing being part of a drama. When you put in the effort, there is so much to appreciate. I've learnt a lot about how I carry and present myself — it's been a form of self-reflection alongside expression. I hope the audience takes away that there is subtle magic in the scenes around us, from love to loss and everything in between."
Jamie
Singer, Dancer & Actor
"I've been in drama for five years, so Lit Night finally gave me an outlet to show my creativity. Being a student director isn't just about having fun with ideas — it means managing a team, thinking on the spot, and learning how to convey your vision to actors who may be brand new. I hope the audience takes away that productions don't have to come from inherently talented or extroverted people. Enough creativity and hard work is enough."
Huan Yie
Student Director & Actor
"Lit Night has been quite fun — I got to meet new people and step out of my comfort zone. I've learned that I shouldn't be scared to try new things. I hope the audience picks up some of the moral teachings woven into the performances."
Arregent
Performer
"I've learned a lot about being more professional as a performer — stage discipline, respecting the stage as a safe space to make mistakes and improve. We all come from different CCAs, yet we're collaborating on something bigger than any one single act. Lit Night, to me, reflects the collective talent of the literature community — it creates a space where different artists can make art freely."
Bryan
Actor
"Being part of the wardrobe and makeup team has been a very meaningful experience. Through this, I gained a deeper appreciation for how much effort goes into running a show. Lit Night was my first experience being involved in something theatre-related, and it gave me the opportunity to see my friends' talents in a completely new light."
Aarthy
Makeup IC
"We've all been working very hard and I can't wait to see it all play out in front of a big audience. As a science-stream student in a STEM CCA, Lit Night lets my arts side shine through. It makes me feel more connected to myself, and I'll cherish the friendships and laughs from this for a long time."
Azraa
Emcee (Hera) & Performer
"Lit Night is a lively, creative space where Literature students come together to celebrate the beauty of stories. I've gained a much deeper appreciation for the amount of work that goes into staging a show. Being Wardrobe IC pushed me to step up, coordinate with others, and ensure our work supported each performance's vision."
Amira
Wardrobe IC
"I think that Lit Night is about showcasing people's talent, expression, and style through drama. To me, it is a once in a lifetime experience since most of us have never been part of a play before. It's a learning experience in the best way possible. I also think that Lit Night is about resilience, a willingness to learn, but most importantly, a time to have fun."
Kimi
Performer
"I hope they will understand that for merely 1.5 hours of a brilliant performance to be put up, it requires no less than 2 months worth of effort. Great things don't come by easily — sometimes other than the end point, it is important to appreciate the process, because often we learn a lot more from our processes than the end result."
Evans
Backstage & Props Crew
"The expression of feelings, sentiments, and larger issues through this form of art — theatre. I think it's so beautiful and fun."
Lily
Singer & Actor
